SharkJaw hydraulic shark jaw systems are purpose-built chain and wire stoppers for anchor handling tug supply (AHTS) vessels, ocean-going tugboats, and offshore support vessels. The shark jaw holds the towing wire or anchor chain securely during operations and releases it instantly on command — a function that is critical to crew safety and operational efficiency in anchor handling, platform supply, and emergency towing work.

Our range covers 25 MT to 700 MT SWL and is available in two configurations. The **Hydraulic Triplex Type** uses three interlocking jaw segments that distribute load across a wider bearing surface, making it the preferred choice for larger chain diameters and heavy AHTS work. The **Fork Type (Closed)** is a compact two-piece design suited to smaller tugboats and vessels with restricted deck space. Both types accept round-link anchor chain and wire rope with appropriate chain roller or wire guide arrangements.

Key design features across the range include hydraulic quick-release operation from both local (deck) and remote (wheelhouse) control stations, interchangeable hardened jaw insert plates for rapid replacement without welding or hot work, flush-deck mounting option to preserve clear deck passage, marine-grade two-coat epoxy paint system as standard (with additional coatings available for Arctic or tropical environments), and PLC-based control with visual and audible status indicators. All units are factory load-tested to 1.25× SWL and supplied with a class society-witnessed test certificate.
